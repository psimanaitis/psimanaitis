2011

When I was in 11th grade, I created a spam bot using iMacros (a predecessor to Selenium). It sent invites with referral links for the web-based RPG I was playing. The iMacros code was generated using Free Pascal and had two parts: data scraping and invitation sending. This is how I learned a lot about how the web works, how to protect against bots, and how to bypass those protections.

For example, Travian had email confirmation and a 20-message-per-5-minutes limit. My script automatically created multiple accounts (using 10 Minute Mail or similar services), confirmed them, sent the allowed amount of messages, then cleared cookies, and logged in with other accounts. By the time the 5-minute limit ran out for the first account, it would reuse previously confirmed accounts. I also learned about proxies.

The script sent over half a million invites a night. It was an "aha" moment that later defined my career choices.

Technologies used: free pascal, iMacros.

Team size: 1 developer.
